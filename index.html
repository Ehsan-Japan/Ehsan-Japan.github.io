About

I am a second-year physics master student in the Department of Quantum System Electronics at Osaka University, Japan. I do research on automated tuning of gate-defined quantum dots. I am advised by Dr.Takafumi Fujita and Professor Akira Oiwa.

Projects

Time-Efficient Method of Tuning a Quadruple Quantum Dot into Single Dot Regime by using ray-based method

Developed a Long Short-Term Memory neural network to form single dots in Ray-based Classification framework. This method effectively tunes a GaAs quadruple quantum dot into four separate single-dot regimes, addressing scalability challenges in quantum dot systems by minimizing the need for manual tuning

Automatic virtual gate extraction of simulated DQD stability diagrams

Developed an algorithm to extract virtual gates of a Double Quantum dot stability diagram based on features extracted by Python line segment detector (PyLsd)
